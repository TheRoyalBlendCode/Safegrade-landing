This is one of the most searched comparisons among UK students in 2026 — but it's based on a misunderstanding. ChatGPT and SafeGrade are not alternatives. They do completely different things.

These are completely different tools

ChatGPT is a generative AI model. You give it a prompt and it produces text. The output is AI-generated content.

SafeGrade is a pre-submission checker. You give it your finished essay and it analyses whether the essay will pass academic scrutiny. It doesn't generate content.

Why using ChatGPT to write essays is risky in 2026

The risks of submitting ChatGPT-generated content
Turnitin detects it. Raw ChatGPT output scores 80–95% on Turnitin's AI detection. Even heavily edited output often scores 40–60%.
ChatGPT fabricates references. Citations generated by ChatGPT frequently don't exist — wrong authors, wrong years, invented DOIs.
It's academic misconduct at virtually every UK university. Penalties range from zero marks to expulsion.
SafeGrade will flag it. SafeGrade's Deep Scan achieves 99.7% accuracy on AI-generated essays.

The smarter approach to using AI at university

  • Use ChatGPT to understand, not to write. Ask it to explain a theory, summarise a debate, or suggest counterarguments. Then write the essay yourself.
  • Use SafeGrade's Essay Coach for essay-specific questions. It knows your subject area and assignment brief. It doesn't write for you — it helps you write better.
  • Check before you submit. If you've used AI in any part of your drafting process, run SafeGrade's AI Risk Check before submission.
The bottom line

ChatGPT generates content. SafeGrade checks content. They're not alternatives. The safe approach is to write your own essays and use SafeGrade to check them.
